Blog

The Terracotta Army is an incredible archaeological discovery in Xi'an, China, containing thousands of life-sized terracotta figures created over 2,000 years ago to protect the first emperor of China in the afterlife. The site is a must-see wonder for visitors to China, and one of the most popular tourist attractions in the country.

The figures in the Terracotta Army are each unique, with individual features and clothing. Originally painted in bright colors, the paint has mostly faded over time, but the level of detail and craftsmanship in each figure is still impressive. Visitors can explore the various pits where the figures were discovered and learn about the history of the site at the on-site museum.

Plan for a full day trip to the Terracotta Army, starting with Pit 1, the largest and most impressive collection of figures. From there, visitors can explore the other pits or continue to the on-site museum. The site is located about an hour's drive from Xi'an and can be easily accessed by taxi or guided tour.

Overall, the Terracotta Army is an unforgettable experience that offers a glimpse into the incredible achievements of ancient China. Don't miss the chance to see this wonder for yourself and marvel at the intricate details and unique features of each terracotta figure.
